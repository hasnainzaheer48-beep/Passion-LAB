ART

To me, art is the ultimate form of human connection, a bridge between the internal landscape of my mind and the external world. This bond began as a childhood obsession with drawing, a time when my hands were constantly iching to make something and every blank surface was an invitation to create. I grew up mesmerized by shows like M.A.D. on Pogo, where watching the magic of DIY creation made me realize that art wasn't just something you looked at,it was something you built with your own two hands. Those episodes were my first real masterclasses, sparking a lifelong habit of sketching that has never truly left me. I see art as a restless dialogue between the creator and the observer, a space where the intangible finally takes shape. Whether through a bold line or a delicate detail, art allows me to speak when words feel insufficient, offering a sanctuary for the imagination.
I love to draw whenever I get the chance, wether it be during lecture or exam. You will find more drawings than actual study material on my notes if you ever happen to look at my register. I like to draw stylized characters and then I try to bend a story around them.
This is one hobby in which I have truly invested my time and focus in. Nothing can replace the feeling of filling a blank page with Storkes of pens.
